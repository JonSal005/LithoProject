Introduction

Lithotripsy is a medical treatment by which kidney stone or other calculus is broken into small particles that can be passed out by the body.

Lithotripsy is the most common treatment in the US for kidney stones.

Kidney stones form when your urine contains more crystal-forming substances — such as calcium, oxalate and uric acid — than the fluid in your urine can dilute.

Kidney stones affect aproximately 1 in 10 people in the US every year. Each year, more than half a million people go to emergency rooms for kidney stone problems.

Types of Lithotripsy

There are several different types of Lithotripsy procedures:

  • Extra Corporeal Shockwave Lithotripsy
  • Laser Lithotripsy
  • Ultrasonic Lithotripsy
  • Electrohydraulic Lithotripsy
  • Robotic Assisted Kidney Stone Removal

There are multiple determining factors that go into which type of Lithotripsy procedure is used, including stone size, stone location within the body/ureter, and stone density.

Extra Corporeal Shockwave Lithotripsy

Extra Corporeal Shockwave Lithotripsy (ESWL) is a procedure to break up stones inside the urinary tract, bile ducts or pancreatic duct with a series of shockwaves generated by a machine called a lithotripter.

ESWL is one of the most common forms of lithotripsy, with success rates of 60-99%. The procedure is completely non-invasive.

Modulith SLX F2 Lithotripter

One of the most common Lithotripters used to perform ESWL surgeries is the Modulith SLX F2.

There are specific parameters used with the Lithotripter in order to treat kidney stones, for example:

Shocks:2400 Energy:3.0 Focal Spot:Precise Hz:1.5
Laser Lithotripsy

Holmium Laser Lithotripsy is a procedure in which a flexible laser fiber is inserted through a scope (camera) placed in the urinary tract via the urethra to break up the stones. The stone fragments are then removed with a small basket or other instruments.

Laser Lithotripsy is one of the most common forms of lithotripsy, with success rates of 50-92.9%. The procedure is done without any incisions.

Holmium YAG Laser

One of the most common Urology Lasers used to perform Laser Lithotripsy surgeries is the Holmium YAG Laser

There are specific parameters used with the Laser in order to treat kidney stones, for example:

Watts/Avg.Pwr:6.4 Hz:8 Energy(Joules):0.8 Total Energy Used:650
